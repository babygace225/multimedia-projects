THE VOYEUR
A flash installation that viewers are like voyeurs watching others' life by using a binocular.
view flash
ROLE

Individual Project

SKILL

Animation

DATE

December 2016

The concept of this project is based on “Voyeur”. The city is noisy but most people in it are lonely. This project mimics the feeling of guilt but also a kind of strange excitement when you are watching somebody secretly. This is inspired by a HBO project that there are many windows for voyeurs to view and what I did is a small version of it, which is just the stories behind one window. The lady was working, dancing, partying, or hugging with her lover.

The whole experience is like an exhibition that viewers stand very far from a big screen and watch it one by one through binoculars. When one person approaches and hold the binocular, the screen changes to the moving shadow window so he/she could use the binocular to view, and after he/she puts it down and leaves, the screen will go back to a moving animation with a shape of window. All the things are trying to create an atmosphere that you are watching somebody without permission and only you can feel this sense of guilt right now.

THE ARTISTRY FADING AWAY
A series of photos about skillful artists and mechanics, taken at Urumqi, some of them by iPhone.
ROLE

Photographer

SKILL

Photography

DATE

December 2014

When I traveled in Xinjiang, I lived in a friend's house in Urumqi. One time I went shopping with her and encountered these old craftsmen. They were making leather, clay, and ice cream and carving gourds by hand. I think these people are ordinary yet unique, because in this modern society, seldom can we see these old ways of doing things. In this distant place, these techniques have been preserved, and some people make a living by using them and continuing these crafts. Seeing the contentment and satisfaction in their eyes and actions, I was instantly impressed and touched. At that time, I did not bring a dedicated camera, but I picked up my iPhone and captured the moment.

In the cities, people feel that these old ways are incompatible, but in this setting, everything seemed very natural. Nowadays, we rely on machines to do almost everything for us. These older cultures are slowly fading away from us, while young people of the new generation are becoming keen on various foreign cultures. Sometimes we really need to pause and think about what we really need to inherit.

THE INVISIBLE
An audio walk experience leading you to another world.
experience it
ROLE

Individual Project

SKILL

Experience Design

DATE

September 2016

The audio is basically about a story bringing you to the year 2046, when the place where you are standing at becomes a fantastic amusement park. A custodian in the park is the key and clew of the whole experience, who leads you to visit the park.

The custodian who talks to you is invisible. You cannot see him but he is there acoustically. What I am trying to convey in this work is that there are actually many “invisibles” around you. You never notice them. However, someday in the future, they might become into a fantasy that you’ve never imagined.

When you press the shutter of the camera, you will be brought to 2046, but you can only hear that paralleled world, which means you are able to hear all the sounds from the same place in 2046 but the place you are in actually is still that one, so all the things in front of you won’t change. When you press the shutter again, your audio world would be brought back to 2016. The meaning of wearing sunglasses is to imply and create an illusion that you are in another world, which could also be regarded as the boundary of real world and the fantasy world.

The video recorded the experience of my participant testing the audio. A camera was put on his head during the whole experience and he started it by playing the audio and follow his mind. The goal of this project is to see how audio alone can affect one person’s action and emotion (call up imagination & memory), and to make people come to look into those invisibles that they usually neglect.

THE YELLOW WINDMILL
A mini film inspired from a real story.
view video
ROLE

Director & Producer

SKILL

Video & Audio Production

DATE

November 2014

The inspiration for the work was drawn from a true story of one of our classmates. During our freshman year, her father died unexpectedly in an accident. As her friends, we decided to make a short film for her to make her feel the warmth of friendship and give her long-lasting comfort. The video tells the story of a female student, Xiaoxiao, who is constantly immersed in misery from her father's death.

Feeling alienated from everything around her, even her own mother, the paper windmill left by her father became a strong emotional symbol for Xiaoxiao. Her mother wanted her daughter to move beyond the pain as soon as possible to re-start their lives, and so she tells Xiaoxiao to throw away the paper windmill, but this request caused Xiaoxiao to withdraw further. One night, Xiaoxiao discovered her mother's secret, realizing that her mother is burdened with a greater but more concealed pain than her. She finally decided to live for the people who are still alive and in her own way say farewell to her father. As is said at the end of the film, suffering is a part of our life, and we are powerful when we learn to accept life the way it is.

This work won the first prize at the 12th Zhejiang Province multimedia contest in the digital video group. I am the director, involved in screenwriting, most parts of shooting, and some parts of editing. I learned real teamwork from the production process, including the allocation of work and project coordination. In addition, our team of five people raised money to purchase a small track in order to get better shots. We also gave up holidays going to another city to shoot. Because our funding was so minimal, we tried to use all of our resources available. For instance, the home scene is my home, and the actress’s mother is my mother. We edited with Adobe premiere CS7 software, and we used a ratio of 2.35:1 – super widescreen like a theater movie. The devices I used might not be the most advanced, but the emotion I expressed was most genuine.
